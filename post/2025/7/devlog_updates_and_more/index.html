Devlog, Updates, And More

P: 30/07/2025



Hello, everyone!


I'm well aware of the fact that I haven't written anything here for well over a month, and that is because I've been hard at work on not just my game but also my website, which I have enriched with a plethora of both functional and stylistic improvements.


So, firstly, regarding my game, Project Debut, the idea at first was for the player to just be a floating pair of hands, for which I made all the models, textures, and animations; however, recently, I decided to scrap that entirely and opt-in to use a full-body model, which I have finally finished. I started by making a base human model and then added modular attachments to the model to add diversity between different characters without having to remodel them every time. Now, all that is left to do is to add the character textures to the player model and animate all the different poses, movements, and actions, which shouldn't be too difficult, as the hardest part, being modeling and rigging, is already done. It is a slight shame how I have wasted a few months on the 'floating arms' idea before scrapping it altogether, but I truly believe that this was the right call to make.


As for my website, when I initially wrote the code for it, I wanted it to look modern with a tint of 'retro amateur,' which I think I succeeded at; however, since then, I decided to lean more towards 'modern' and 'professional' than 'goofy' and 'amateur'; thus, I completely revamped some aspects while slightly adjusting others. You can take a gander at how my old website design looked:




And how my new website design looks:




From the images, you can see that the navigation bar has been completely changed in favor of a smaller, more compact, and minimalistic design. The footer has been adjusted for a more mobile-friendly design with the intent for all its text and links to be at the center for ease of access. The text selection color has also been changed from a solid black color to a transparent magenta, which, personally, adds more 'zest' to it. Finally, the biggest change made is the actual home page. Before, the whole homepage was basically a massive infodump, while in the new version, I compressed the main parts from the first version into three simple and intuitive colored cards.
